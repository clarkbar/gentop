That is, if \(\phi \colon V \to \RR^n\) is a linear isomorphism, then \(U \subseteq V\) is open if and only if \(\phi(V) \subseteq \RR^n\) is open. As an exercise, you can show that the topology is the same, irrespective of which isomorphism \(\phi\) you select. (Hint: linear isomorphisms from \(\RR^n\) to itself are always homeomorphisms!)
