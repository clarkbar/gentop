Let \(S \subseteq \PP^1_{\RR}\text{,}\) and let \(L \in \PP^1_{\RR}\) be a line that is close to \(\tau(S)\text{.}\) We aim to show that \(L\) is close to \(S\text{;}\) so let \(\varepsilon>0\text{.}\) There exists a line \(L' \in \tau(S)\) such that either \(\|i(L) - i(L')\|\lt \varepsilon\) or \(\|i(L)-j(L')\|\lt \varepsilon/2\text{.}\) Accordingly, there exists a line \(L'' \in S\) such that either \(\|i(L') - i(L'')\| \lt \varepsilon/2\) or \(\|i(L') - j(L'')\| \lt \varepsilon/2\text{.}\) Analyzing the four options and applying the triangle inequality, we find that either \(\|i(L) - i(L'')\|\lt \varepsilon\) or \(\|i(L) - j(L'') \|\lt \varepsilon\text{.}\)
