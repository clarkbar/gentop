Proposition 1.1.4.

Let \(X \subseteq \RR^n\) be a subspace.

  1. For every subset \(S \subseteq X\text{,}\) every point of \(S\) is close to \(S\text{.}\) That is, \(S \subseteq \tau_X(S)\text{.}\)

  2. No points of \(X\) are close to \(\varnothing\text{.}\) That is, \(\tau_X(\varnothing) = \varnothing\text{.}\)

  3. If \(S_1,\dots,S_n \subseteq X\) are a finite collection of subsets, then a point \(x \in X\) is close to \(S_1 \cup \dots \cup S_n\) if and only if, for some \(i\text{,}\) the point \(x\) is close to \(S_i\text{.}\) That is,

    \begin{equation*} \tau_X\left(S_1 \cup \dots \cup S_n\right) = \tau_X(S_1) \cup \dots \cup \tau_X(S_n)\text{.} \end{equation*}

  4. If \(S \subseteq X\text{,}\) then if \(x \in X\) is a point that is close to \(\tau_X(S)\text{,}\) then \(x\) is close to \(S\) itself. That is, \(\tau_X(\tau_X(S)) = \tau_X(S)\text{.}\)
