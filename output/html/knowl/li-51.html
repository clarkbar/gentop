Item 1.

When we think about the topological nature of a subspace \(X \subseteq \RR^n\text{,}\) the ambient Euclidean space in which we find them isn't all that important; homeomorphic subspaces can be found in wildly different Euclidean spaces. The only thing we do want to remember from the ambient \(\RR^n\) is the definition of the closure operator \(\tau_X\text{.}\)
