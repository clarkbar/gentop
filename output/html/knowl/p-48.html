If \(x\) is close to \(\tau_X(S)\text{,}\) then for any \(\varepsilon >0\text{,}\) we may select \(t\in \tau_X(S)\) such that \(d(x,t) \lt \varepsilon/2\text{.}\) Since \(t\) is close to \(S\text{,}\) we may select \(s \in S\) such that \(d(t, s) \lt \varepsilon/2\text{.}\) Now by the triangle inequality,

\begin{equation*} d(x, s) \leq d(x, t) + d(t, s) \lt \varepsilon\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus \(x\) is close to \(S\text{.}\)
