We aim to show that \(X \smallsetminus U\) is closed; hence let \(x \in U\) be a point that is close to \(X \smallsetminus U\text{.}\) For some \(\varepsilon>0\text{,}\) the intersection \(B^n(x,\varepsilon) \cap X\) is contained in \(U\text{.}\) But at the same time, since \(x\) is close to \(X \smallsetminus U\text{,}\) the intersection \(B^n(x, \varepsilon) \cap (X \smallsetminus U) \neq \varnothing\text{.}\) This is a contradiction that shows that no point of \(U\) is close to \(X \smallsetminus U\text{;}\) thus \(U\) is open.
