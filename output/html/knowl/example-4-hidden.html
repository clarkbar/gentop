Let's consider examples in \(\RR\) in which the finiteness hypotheses of the previous proposition are necessary.

  • Infinite unions of closed subsets need not be closed:

    \begin{equation*} \bigcup_{n\in\NN^{\ast}} \left[-1+\frac{1}{n}, 1-\frac{1}{n}\right] = \left]-1,1\right[\text{,} \end{equation*}

    or open:

    \begin{equation*} \bigcup_{n\in\NN^{\ast}} \left[0,1-\frac{1}{n}\right] = \left[0,1\right[ .\text{.} \end{equation*}

  • Infinite intersections of open subsets need not be open:

    \begin{equation*} \bigcap_{n\in\NN^{\ast}} \left]-1-\frac{1}{n}, 1+\frac{1}{n}\right[ = [-1,1]\text{,} \end{equation*}

    or closed:

    \begin{equation*} \bigcap_{n\in\NN^{\ast}} \left]0, 1+\frac{1}{n}\right[ = \left]0,1\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}
