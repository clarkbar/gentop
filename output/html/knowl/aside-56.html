The phrase “there is a unique \(x\) such that \(\phi(x)\)” — sometimes written \((\exists ! x)\ \phi(x)\) — is a helpful shorthand for the longer sentence

\begin{equation*} (\exists x)(\phi(x) \wedge ((\forall y)(\phi(y){\implies}(x=y))))\text{.} \end{equation*}

You knew that, of course, but our point here is that it's a formula of predicate calculus, and so the phrase “such-and-so is a map” is a formula of predicate calculus as well.
