If \(S_1,\dots,S_n \subseteq X\) are a finite collection of subsets, then a point \(x \in X\) is close to \(S_1 \cup \dots \cup S_n\) if and only if, for some \(i\text{,}\) the point \(x\) is close to \(S_i\text{.}\) That is,

\begin{equation*} \tau_X\left(S_1 \cup \dots \cup S_n\right) = \tau_X(S_1) \cup \dots \cup \tau_X(S_n)\text{.} \end{equation*}
