Indeed, if \(U' \subseteq \RR^n\) is an open subset, then let \(Z'\) denote its complement. Since \(Z'\cap X\) is closed by what we've already proved, its complement in \(X\text{,}\) which is \(U' \cap X\) is open. Conversely, if \(U \subseteq X\) is open, then let \(Z\) denote its complement in \(X\text{.}\) By what we've already shown, there is a closed subset \(Z' \subseteq \RR^n\) such that \(Z = Z' \cap X\text{,}\) so if \(U'\) denotes the open complement of \(Z'\text{,}\) then \(U = U'\cap X\text{.}\)
